Peter Vroemen

Bio

My work experience started as a Draftsperson working with town planing and lands in the Yellowknife office of the government of the North West Territories. One of the more interesting projects was drawing up early proposals for the boundry lines to create a new region called Nunavut.

Returning to the south and retraining as a carpenter I quickly learned how structures fit together almost sculptural in geometric terms, with a strong emphasis on functionality. Once you completed the house the goal was to live in it comfortably and efficiently.

From carpentry I moved to outdoor activities working with the National Capital Commission to maintain parks and recreational areas in the Ottawa region. Eventually working at the prime minister's residence as acting head gardener overseeing the planting and maintenance of thousands of annuals and perennials yearly.

My interest in art has been a search, with attempts to explore the uses of clay, wood, oil, watercolor, pen and ink and to capture something of the ever evolving and joyous experience that is art.

Silk screen printing has opened doors of perception for me with endless possibilities to create and express what I see and how I see it.

Formal Training

1977: Arts and Crafts program at Algonquin College with a specialized in Metallurgy, Color theory, and Life drawing.
1980-2020: Ottawa school of Art.
Etching courses with Leonard Gerbrant and Rob Hinchley.
Four years of silk screen printing with Farouk Kaspaules.
